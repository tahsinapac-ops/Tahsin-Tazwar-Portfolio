Shared OTB pavilion at GITEX Global 2026

Book one Bangladesh pavilion in Dubai, fill it with partner companies, and split the floor and preparation costs between them. OTB adds a service charge as a separate line. This is the model PSEB runs for Pakistan — proposed here privately, without waiting for government to organise it.

Decisions we need from you

1Service charge — 25% or 50%? The published flyer says 50% of cost share; this model assumes 25%. At ten companies that is $7,500 versus $9,000 per partner, and 50% puts us above the bare-stall price.
2Who signs the pavilion contract? Space must be booked and deposited before we know the final company count. If we sell eight slots and contract for twenty, OTB carries the gap.
3Minimum viable group — do we commit to seven? Below seven the offer is not defensible. Recommend a go/no-go gate at seven signed slots with deposits returned if unmet.
4Subsidy — who applies, and what do we assume? All figures assume 40% secured via EPB / ICT Division export promotion. At 0% the ten-company fee rises to $12,500 and the concept fails.
5Is seven weeks enough to sell the slots? Enrolment must close 7 September to leave three months of preparation. That is the tightest constraint in the plan.

Principal risks

1Under-subscription. The shared pool is fixed once contracted, so a short group raises everyone's fee — the opposite of the promise we are making.
2Cost basis unverified. The $60k pool is a planning estimate. No pavilion quote has been obtained.
3Subsidy is not committed. Pursuing it is not securing it, and partners will read 40% as a promise.
4Delivery load. Buyer matchmaking, training and twelve weeks of follow-up are what justify the service charge — and are the hardest part to staff.

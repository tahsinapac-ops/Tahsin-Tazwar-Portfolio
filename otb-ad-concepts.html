Visual Advertising · outsourcetobd.com

Five ways to sell trust, not cheap labour.

The product is not Bangladesh. The product is a decision a foreign buyer is nervous about, de-risked. Every concept below turns on the same insight from the OTB positioning — talent is not the gap, verified trust is.

Audience
CTOs, procurement leads & founders — US, EU, Gulf
Format
30–45s film, 16:9 + 9:16 cutdowns
Channels
LinkedIn, YouTube pre-roll, GITEX booth loop
Proposition
The trusted gateway to verified BD tech companies

The Concepts

01

The Tab You Didn't Close

Recommended Low budget

Screen-life realism · 35s · one actor, one location

Plot
  1. 11pm, dark office. A CTO stares at twelve browser tabs — freelancer profiles, agency landing pages, a Reddit thread titled "has anyone actually outsourced to…"
  2. They close them one by one. The sound design is a countdown.
  3. Cursor hovers over the last tab: a verified OTB company profile — real team photo, audit badge, named client, delivery record.
  4. They don't close it. Laptop shuts. Cut to morning: same face on a video call, laughing with a Dhaka team.
Why it works
Sells the before feeling — decision fatigue and fear of getting burned — instead of selling a country. The product becomes the one tab that survived the cull.
Look
Screen-recording realism, cold blue monitor glow → warm daylight at the turn. No music until the last tab.
End card
"Stop shortlisting strangers."
OutsourceToBD.com — verified Bangladeshi tech companies.
02

Split Screen / Same Hands

Silent-autoplay proof

Wordless visual argument · 30s · travels across every language

Plot
  1. A hard vertical split holds for the entire film.
  2. Left: a product in use — a fintech app in Dubai, a hospital dashboard in Berlin, a logistics scanner in Toronto.
  3. Right: the exact matching moment being built in Dhaka. Same gesture, same rhythm, hands mirroring across the seam.
  4. Final shot: the seam dissolves. It was one room all along.
Why it works
No VO needed until the last line, so it lands in a muted feed. Argues by rhyme rather than by claim.
Line
"You've already been using it."
03

The Verification

Highest trust weight

Documentary, no actors · 45s · doubles as sales-deck & booth asset

Plot
  1. Follow one real onboarding: the OTB team walks into a Bangladeshi company's office.
  2. They check delivery records, meet the engineers, sit in on a stand-up, sign off.
  3. Intercut with a buyer on the other side of the world reading that same finished profile.
  4. Hard cut to type: "127 companies applied. 41 are listed."
Why it works
Scarcity and rejection are the strongest trust signals in a market where trust is the entire problem. This is the film that converts skeptical procurement.
Reuse
Cuts down into the GITEX pavilion loop and the partner proposal deck already in production.
04

Time Zones

Low budget

Light and shareable · 20s · one prop, one match-cut engine

Plot
  1. 6pm London — a founder closes the laptop mid-problem.
  2. 11pm Dhaka — the work continues under different light.
  3. 8am London — the fix is already in the inbox.
  4. Every cut lands on the same coffee cup: filled, emptied, filled.
Line
"Your night shift already clocked in."
05

Five Frames

Ship this week

Static carousel · no shoot · OTB dark plate + gold type

Frames
  1. "You wouldn't hire an engineer without a reference."
  2. "So why hire an agency without one?"
  3. "Every company on OutsourceToBD is verified in person."
  4. Three client logos + one hard number.
  5. CTA — Browse verified companies →
Why it works
Costs nothing, tests the message before you commit a production budget to concepts 01–03.

Production Note

Cheap
01 and 04 shoot for almost nothing — screen capture plus a single location and one performer.
Valuable
03 needs real access to a member company, but it is the highest-value asset in the set: one shoot yields the ad, the sales-deck video and the GITEX booth loop.
Sequence
Run 05 as a message test → produce 01 as the paid-media film → shoot 03 alongside the next verification visit.

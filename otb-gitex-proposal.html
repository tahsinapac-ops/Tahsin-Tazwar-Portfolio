The honest diagnosis

You don't have a lead problem.

You have an access, preparation and follow-up problem. The buyers are already at these events. What stops Bangladeshi firms winning them is everything that happens before and after the four days on the floor.

Access

The room is expensive

A prime-location stall is priced for companies with marketing budgets ten times yours. Book late and you get a corner nobody walks past.

Preparation

Nobody knows you're coming

Arriving with a banner and hope is not a strategy. Buyers plan their four days weeks in advance — if you are not on their calendar, you are not in their week.

Follow-up

Deals close after the event

Most leads collected on the floor are never worked properly. The contract is won in the six weeks that follow, not at the stall.

How the money works

Three separate lines. Nothing hidden inside anything else.

You are never quoted one lump figure. Your invoice always shows what is shared, what is yours alone, and what OTB charges for the work — as three distinct lines.

Line 1

Event & pavilion cost

Floor space, pavilion design and build, branding, AV, freight, utilities and on-site crew.

↓ Split equally between every attending company

Line 2

Preparation cost

Event data room, buyer contact lists, market intelligence, matchmaking desk, the 3-part training programme and the post-event follow-up desk.

↓ Split equally between every attending company

Line 3

OTB service charge

What OTB earns for six months of organising, negotiating, chasing subsidies, and running the follow-up. Charged as a flat percentage of your net cost share.

→ Added separately, never buried in the costs above

Government funding is pursued on your behalf. OTB applies for export-promotion subsidy against the pavilion and preparation costs. Whatever is secured is deducted from your cost share before the service charge is calculated — so a bigger subsidy cuts the service charge too.
